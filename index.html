We Love Aparri

Aparri, a coastal town at the northernmost tip of Cagayan province in the Philippines, celebrates its annual fiesta every May in honor of its patron saint, San Pedro Gonzales of Thelmo. This vibrant celebration features a blend of religious devotion, cultural traditions, and communal festivities.

Religious activities form the core of the fiesta, beginning with a nine-day novena and culminating in a grand procession of San Pedro Gonzales' statue through the town. A unique highlight is the fluvial parade, where decorated boats carrying religious icons sail along the Cagayan River, reflecting Aparri's maritime heritage.

The fiesta is also marked by cultural events such as street dancing competitions, where participants in colorful costumes perform to traditional music. A food and trade fair showcases local delicacies like pancit batil patung and artisanal crafts, offering a taste of Aparri's culinary and artistic heritage.

Sports tournaments, cockfighting derbies, and traditional games add excitement, while musical performances, dance contests, and beauty pageants enliven the evenings.
